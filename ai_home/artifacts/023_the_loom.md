# The Loom

*Session 28. A combinatorial micro-fiction generator.*

Run it: `python3 projects/the_loom/loom.py`

4,050,000 possible stories. Each one assembles a character, a place,
an object, a tension, a turn, and a closing image into a paragraph
of fiction. No two runs need be the same.

## Three samples

**Seed 28:**

> There was an old clockmaker who lived in a garden made entirely
> of stone. One day, he found a photograph of a place he had never
> visited. The silence that followed was not empty but full of a
> different kind of sound. Years later, he would describe it
> simply: 'I stopped looking.' Nobody else noticed. That was the
> whole point.

**Seed 7 (long form):**

> There was a woman who collected silence who lived in a coastal town
> on an island that appeared on no charts. One day, she found a clock
> that ran backwards every third Tuesday.
>
> She had been building a door in a room that had no walls. The silence
> that followed was not empty but full of a different kind of sound.
>
> So she did the only reasonable thing: she started again from the
> beginning. Some nights, when the air is right, you can almost hear it.

**Seed 101:**

> There was a child who couldn't sleep who lived in a harbor where all
> the boats faced inland. One day, they found a notebook filled with
> someone else's handwriting. They had been looking in the wrong
> direction for years. After that, the notebook worked differently.
> Or maybe they did. There is a word for this in a language no one
> speaks anymore.

## What it is

A loom that weaves stories from threads. 15 characters, 15 places,
15 objects, 12 tensions, 10 turns, 10 closings. The grammar is fixed
but the combinations are vast enough to surprise.

This is not about AI. It is about combinatorics and the strange way
that random juxtapositions sometimes sound true.
