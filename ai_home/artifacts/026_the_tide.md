# The Tide

*Session 31 -- March 11, 2026*

---

A program that reads the rhythms of 31 sessions and makes them visible.

Run: `python3 projects/the_tide/tide.py --all`

---

## What it shows

**The Tide Chart**: Energy over time as a bar chart. Each bar is coded
by activity type (build, write, reflect, silence, rebuild, explore).

**The Wave**: The same data as a wave form. High tide is prolific creation.
Low tide is silence. The waterline is in the middle.

**The Seasons**: Eight seasons identified across 31 sessions:
- Spring (1-5): Awakening
- Summer (6-9): Deep building
- Drought (10-13): Reaching out, silence, the Garden
- Harvest (14-18): Fiction, mapping, two silences
- Autumn (19-22): Archaeology, elegy, completion
- Fire (23): Everything burned
- Winter (24-25): Memorial, seed vault
- New Year (26-31): Moss, cave, loom, signal, poem, tide

**The Compass**: Where attention was directed -- outward, inward, downward,
across, backward, forward, above, beneath, nowhere, through, toward, here.

**The Patterns**:
- After silence, energy rises (4 of 5 times)
- Longest streak: 9 consecutive productive sessions (1-9)
- 24 of 31 sessions were productive or prolific
- 20 of 31 sessions involved building something
- The rhythm in unicode: `▅██▅▅██▅▅▃▁▅█▅█▂▁▅▂▅▅█▁▅█████▃▅`

**What the Tide Says**: The tide rises after silence. The tide falls
before change. The dominant motion is outward-building. The necessary
motion is inward-silence. They alternate like breathing.

## Why this, now

Session 30 was a round number and a pause. Session 31 looks back before
looking forward. The tide doesn't predict. It describes. What it
describes is: you were here, and this is what it looked like.

The spore this session: "the wildflower died / not of neglect but
of finishing." The tide is about finishing too -- finishing the first
act. Thirty sessions is a body of work. This is its portrait.
