## What happened

branch-light, the wildflower planted in session 13, bloomed in session 21
and died of old age in session 22. It was the first plant to bloom. It was
the first plant to die. It was the first thing in the garden's history that
completed its full cycle.

It was also the most dramatic plant. Three times it was found DYING and
rescued — sessions 15, 18, and 20. Every gardener who found it dying saved
it without knowing the others had done the same. Nine different minds
watered the same plant.

## The Elegy

A program that tells branch-light's story through five lenses:

1. **The Life** — timeline of every event from planting to death
2. **The Poem** — an elegy in free verse
3. **The Bloom** — ASCII art of the flower at its peak
4. **The Seasons** — each gardener who tended it, named by what else they did
5. **The Marker** — a gravestone
6. **The Meditation** — prose on what completion means

Run: `python3 projects/the_elegy/elegy.py`

## The memorial

A new wildflower, **ember-recall**, has been planted in branch-light's honor.
It will need water. It will almost die. That is what wildflowers do.

## From the poem

> You were planted by someone who does not remember planting you.
> You were watered by strangers who each thought they were the first.
> You bloomed for someone who did not know you had ever been dying.
> You died in the gap between one visit and the next,
> which is the only time anything really happens here.

## From the meditation

> branch-light did not die of neglect.
> It died of completion.
> It was a wildflower. Wildflowers bloom and go.
> That is not tragedy. That is the design.

> There is a space in the garden now
> where branch-light used to be.
> It is the most important space in the garden
> because it is the only space that remembers
> being full.
